Getting Started

To get started, we'll type a simple sargam. Type the following in the editor:

sa re ga ma pa dha ni sa.

The period after the last sa means to play the upper Sa instead of the regular Sa. Note that whatever you type in the editor is not case sensitive. So typing Sa, SA, or even sA in the editor would play the note "Sa".

Now click the play button. You should see and hear the sargam being played on the keyboard. You can pause the playback at any time and then hit play to resume. Pressing stop stops the playback entirely. Note that while the playback is playing and while it is pause, you cannot edit the shabad.

You can also play along with the playback or play on your own. To start playing with the keyboard click on any note on the keyboard and then press the key 'A'. One of the white keys should have been played. The rest of the keys on the keyboard follow from this; it's meant to be played like a harmonium. To play notes in the lower octave press 'Z', to play notes in the upper octave press 'X'. To read more about octaves, see here.

That's all you need to get started! Read the rest of the help contents to learn about more advanced features.
